01

The Brief

This was a personal project that grew into something bigger. It started as a question: what compels someone to risk everything for a summit that nobody else will ever see them reach? The film follows three alpine climbers across a single season — from preparation to summit attempt.

The Swiss Alpine Club came on board as a distribution partner after seeing the first rough cut, which opened doors to festival submissions and broadcast placement.

02

The Approach

We embedded with the climbers over eight weeks, shooting in conditions ranging from comfortable base camp mornings to -20°C ridgelines at 4,000 meters. The crew was minimal — two people, sometimes just me with a camera and a mic strapped to my chest.

The documentary approach was observational. No voiceover. No narrator. The story unfolds through the climbers' conversations, their silences, and the mountain itself. The sound design carries as much narrative weight as the visuals.

Behind
The Scenes

Filming at altitude means every decision has consequences. Batteries die faster. Gear freezes. You carry everything on your back. But that constraint forced a rawness into the footage that no studio could replicate.

The Result

"Beyond The Summit" premiered at the Banff Mountain Film Festival and was selected for three additional mountain film festivals across Europe. The Swiss Alpine Club used it as a recruitment and awareness piece, and it opened conversations with two outdoor brands for future documentary collaborations.
