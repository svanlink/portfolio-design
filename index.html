The Filmmaker

I'm Sebastian — a filmmaker based in Geneva. I've spent the last several years trying to get better at one thing: communicating something honest through a camera. Brand films, commercials, docs — the format matters less to me than whether the thing we make actually feels like something.

I'm interested in creating work that outlasts the moment it was made for. The kind of piece where someone watches it years later and it still feels like a present-tense conversation. That's what I'm chasing. I'm not there yet — but every project gets me a little closer.

I've been fortunate to work with brands like Adidas, Cartier, Red Bull, and Nike — from the Swiss Alps to the volcanic plains of Iceland. Every project teaches me something. I take one thing that went well, one thing to work on, and carry that forward. The rest falls away.

I think the best work comes from building real relationships with the people you create alongside. Not just chasing the next thing — but planting seeds, saying yes to what excites you, and trusting that the through line will reveal itself over time.

Common Questions

01

What does a brand film cost?

It depends on the scope. A one-day shoot with a small crew and basic post-production typically starts around CHF 8,000–15,000. Larger campaigns with multi-day shoots, bigger crews, and full post-production range from CHF 20,000–60,000+. I scope every project individually — every film is different.

02

How long does production take?

From first conversation to final delivery, most projects take 4–8 weeks. Pre-production is usually 1–2 weeks, production is 1–4 days, and post-production takes 2–4 weeks depending on complexity. Rush timelines are possible when the project calls for it.

03

Brand film vs. commercial?

A commercial is built to sell — usually 15–60 seconds, product-focused, designed for paid media. A brand film is built to connect — typically 1–3 minutes, story-driven, and lives on your website, social channels, or at events. Both require the same production quality, but the intent and structure are different.

04

Do you work outside Switzerland?

Yes. I'm based in Geneva but work across Europe and internationally. Recent projects have taken me to the Swiss Alps, Iceland, and across France. Travel and logistics are part of the production plan — I handle that so you don't have to.
