Swiss Athletics

Athlete portrait series — the human side of high performance

Scroll
Client

Swiss Athletics Federation

Role

Director · Cinematographer · Editor

Year

2024

Location

Bern & Basel

01

The Brief

Swiss Athletics wanted to humanize their national team ahead of a major European championship. The federation needed content that went beyond race results — portraits that showed who these athletes are when the cameras aren't rolling.

The series had to work as standalone short films for social, but also cut together into a longer anthology piece for broadcast and event screenings.

02

The Approach

We spent a full day with each athlete — not at the stadium, but in their world. Training grounds, kitchens, commutes. The interviews were unscripted, guided only by a few open questions about sacrifice, fear, and what keeps them running.

The visual language was deliberate: slow, intimate close-ups intercut with wide training shots. We used natural sound as a score — footsteps on track, breath in cold air, the snap of starting blocks. Music only enters in the final act.

The Result

The portrait series ran across Swiss Athletics' social channels in the lead-up to the European Championships. It became their most-shared content of the year and was picked up by SRF for a broadcast feature segment.
