Swiss Athletics

Who are these people when they stop running?

Scroll
Client

Swiss Athletics Federation

Role

Director · Cinematographer · Editor

Year

2024

Location

Bern & Basel

01

The Brief

Swiss Athletics had a problem they couldn't quite name. Their athletes were fast, but nobody knew them. Ahead of a big European championship, they wanted something that went deeper than highlight reels. They wanted people to care about these runners as people.

Each portrait had to stand alone for social, but also stitch together into an anthology for broadcast. That dual constraint shaped everything about how we shot and structured the stories.

02

The Approach

We spent a full day with each athlete, but not at the stadium. We went to their kitchens, their commutes, the quiet places where they think about quitting. The interviews were unscripted. We just asked about sacrifice, fear, and what keeps them going when nobody's clapping.

Visually, we kept it slow. Intimate close-ups on hands, eyes, the small fidgets before a race. Then wide training shots to let the air in. We used natural sound instead of a score — footsteps on track, breath in cold air, the snap of starting blocks. Music only enters at the end, and by then you've already felt the whole thing.

03

The Result

The series ran in the lead-up to the European Championships and became Swiss Athletics' most-shared content that year. SRF picked it up for a broadcast segment, which we didn't expect. But the part that mattered was hearing athletes say they finally felt seen — not as competitors, but as themselves.
